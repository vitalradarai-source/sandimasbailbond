San Dimas Bail Bonds for Domestic Violence Arrests

San Dimas sits at the eastern edge of Los Angeles County, right along the border with San Bernardino County. That geographic position matters when a domestic violence arrest occurs, because it determines which county's bail schedule applies, which jail processes the booking, and which courthouse handles the arraignment. For residents of San Dimas, all of those answers point to Los Angeles County — and understanding that system is essential to securing a fast release after a DV arrest.

San Dimas Is LA County — Not San Bernardino County

Although San Dimas borders San Bernardino County cities like La Verne's eastern edge and is close to communities like Pomona and Claremont that share the county border, San Dimas itself is fully within Los Angeles County. This means that a domestic violence arrest by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department or the San Dimas station area falls under LA County jurisdiction for booking, bail, and courts.

Confusion about county lines is common in this part of the San Gabriel Valley — particularly when family members are calling from a different city and assume the arrest may have gone to a San Bernardino County facility. If the arrest occurred within San Dimas city limits, it is an LA County matter. The LA County bail schedule applies, the LASD facility handles booking, and the Pomona courthouse handles arraignment.

Booking Facility: LASD Industry Station Area

Domestic violence arrests in San Dimas are handled by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department, which serves the city through its Industry Station area coverage. The Industry Station is located at 150 N Hudson Avenue, City of Industry, CA 91744. After a DV arrest in San Dimas, the arrested individual will typically be booked at this facility or transferred to a county jail depending on the charge classification and booking volume.

For felony domestic violence charges under PC § 273.5, processing may route to a county facility. In either case, bail is set according to the LA County bail schedule and is available to post as soon as the booking process is complete. A licensed bail agent familiar with LASD's Industry Station area can begin coordinating the release immediately after booking concludes — no mandatory waiting period exists under California law.

If an Emergency Protective Order was issued at the scene by the arresting deputy, it is a civil contact restriction only. An EPO does not create a hold on the jail booking and does not delay the availability of bail. Release on bail and compliance with the EPO are entirely separate matters — a defendant can be released on bail and remain fully compliant with the EPO by honoring the no-contact terms.

Arraignment: Governor George Deukmejian Courthouse in Pomona

After a San Dimas domestic violence arrest, the first court appearance — the arraignment — takes place at the Governor George Deukmejian Courthouse in Pomona, located at 400 Civic Center Plaza, Pomona, CA 91766. This courthouse serves the eastern San Gabriel Valley and is the designated arraignment venue for criminal matters arising from the LASD Industry Station service area, including San Dimas.

Arraignment for defendants who remain in custody must occur within 48 hours of arrest (excluding weekends and holidays). Defendants released on bail have more flexibility in scheduling, but their first appearance date will be assigned at the time of release and must be honored. Failing to appear after posting bail forfeits the bond and triggers a bench warrant — the single most important thing a co-signer and defendant must understand before the bail paperwork is signed.

California Insurance Code § 1800.4 sets the bail bond premium at a uniform 10% of the total bail amount for all licensed agents in the state. There is no variation between agents on this rate — a $50,000 bail bond carries a $5,000 premium statewide. The premium is the cost of securing the bond and is not a refundable deposit. However, paying the premium and securing the bond means your family member walks out of jail while their case proceeds through the court system — rather than sitting in a county facility for weeks or months awaiting their court date.

Where are domestic violence arrests from San Dimas booked?
San Dimas DV arrests are handled by the LASD Industry Station (150 N Hudson Ave, City of Industry, CA 91744) and may process at that facility or a county jail depending on charge classification.

Which courthouse handles arraignment for San Dimas domestic violence cases?

Does an Emergency Protective Order stop bail from being posted in San Dimas?
No. An EPO is a civil contact restriction issued at the scene. It does not place a legal hold on the booking process and does not prevent bail from being posted from the county schedule.

What is the bail amount for a PC 273.5 felony DV charge in San Dimas?
